# Y Combinator Data Sources

## Primary YC Sources

| Source | URL | Best For |
|--------|-----|----------|
| YC Work at a Startup | https://www.workatastartup.com/companies | Job listings, company snapshots, salary ranges |
| YC Company Directory | https://www.ycombinator.com/companies | Batch, one-liner, website, status |
| YC Demo Day | https://www.ycombinator.com/demo-day | Pitch videos, early traction, founding team |

## Funding & Financials

- **Crunchbase** (crunchbase.com): Funding rounds, investors, valuation, timeline. Free tier enough for basic research.
- **PitchBook** (pitchbook.com): Deeper financials; subscription often via university/employer.
- **Wellfound** (wellfound.com): Jobs, compensation, culture.

## Team & People

- **LinkedIn**: Founder backgrounds, education, previous companies, tenure. Search "[Company] employees" and founder names.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator**: Advanced filtering, hierarchy; premium.

## Product & Market

- **Product Hunt** (producthunt.com): Launches, upvotes, comments, maker responses.
- **G2 / Capterra**: B2B reviews and positioning.
- **BuiltWith** (builtwith.com), **StackShare** (stackshare.io): Tech stack.
- **GitHub**: Org repos, open source, engineering culture.

## Company Health & Culture

- **Glassdoor**, **Blind**: Reviews, salary, interview experiences. Sample sizes vary.
- **X (Twitter)**, **LinkedIn company page**: Recent updates, hiring posts.
- **Hacker News**: Search company name for discussions.

## News & AI-Specific

- **TechCrunch**: Search "[Company] YC" for funding and launches.
- **VentureBeat**, AI news sites: AI sector coverage.
- **arXiv**, **Papers with Code**, **Hugging Face**: Research and models by company/founders.

## Research Order (Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3)

1. YC directory → basic info  
2. Work at a Startup → jobs  
3. Company website → product and team  
4. Crunchbase → funding  
5. LinkedIn → founders and team  
6. GitHub → tech and culture  
7. Product Hunt → market reception  
8. Glassdoor / X → culture and updates  
9. News search → press and recent developments  

Use Tier 1 for every company; add Tier 2–3 for deeper analysis or application strategy.
